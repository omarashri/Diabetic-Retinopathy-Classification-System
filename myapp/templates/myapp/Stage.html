Unfortunately, You're diseased :(

This is the final stage of diabetic retinopathy. New blood vessels proliferate, growing inside the retina and into the vitreous gel, which is the fluid that fills the eye. Because these blood vessels are delicate, they may begin to leak and bleed. As a result, scar tissue may form, causing retinal detachment, the pulling away of the retina from underlying tissue. Retinal detachment may cause spotty vision, flashes of lights, or severe vision loss.
